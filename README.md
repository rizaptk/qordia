# ‚òï Customer Experience Flow

## 1. Arriving at the Seating Area

When customers sit at a table or seating area, they will find a unique QR code placed on the table or near the seating location. Each QR code is linked to a specific table or seating zone inside the caf√© or food court system.

This allows Qordia to automatically identify:

- The customer‚Äôs seating location

Customers do not need to install any application. Scanning the QR code opens the digital ordering interface directly in their mobile browser.

## 2. Opening the Digital Menu

After scanning the QR code, customers are redirected to the Qordia ordering page where they can:

- Browse categorized menus (coffee, beverages, snacks, meals, desserts)
- View images, descriptions, and pricing
- Check item availability in real time
- Customize orders (sugar level, ice level, toppings, milk options, spice level, add-ons)

Menus are designed to be mobile-friendly, fast-loading, and easy to navigate ‚Äî ideal for quick ordering environments like coffee shops and food courts.

---

## 3. Adding Items to the Order

Customers can select multiple items and adjust quantities before adding them to their order cart.

The cart allows customers to:

- Review selected items
- Modify quantities
- Add special preparation notes
- See estimated total pricing

This helps reduce ordering mistakes and ensures preparation accuracy for drink and food customization.

---

## 4. Confirming the Order

Once customers confirm their order:

- The order is instantly sent to the caf√© or preparation station
- The order is automatically linked to the correct table or seating zone
- The system assigns timestamps and order tracking status

Customers may optionally:

- Enter their name
- Place group orders
- Select payment preference (if enabled)

---

## 5. Order Tracking

Customers can monitor their order progress in real time through status updates such as:

- Order received
- Preparing drinks or food
- Ready for pickup or serving
- Completed

This reduces customer uncertainty and minimizes the need to repeatedly ask staff about order progress.
